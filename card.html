Honey Badger

The Honey Badger is also known as ratel; Ratel is an Afrikaans word, possibly derived from the Middle Dutch word raat for honeycomb (either because of its cry or its taste for honey).

  • Scientific Name: Mellivora capensis
  • Average Length: 55-77 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 24 years
  • Habitat: Africa, Middle East, Indian subcontinent

The honey badger has a fairly long body, but is distinctly thick-set and broad across the back. Its skin is remarkably loose, and allows it to turn and twist freely within it.
